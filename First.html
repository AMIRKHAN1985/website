Data Types:¶

Numeric Data Types:¶

1. Integer (int) Integers are whole numbers without decimal point. They can be positive or negative as long as they dont contain a decimal point that would make a number a floating number, a distinct numeric type. Integers have unlimited size in Python 3.

Examples: 793, -254, 4

In [ ]:
a=20
print(type(a))

<class 'int'>

2. Floating-point numbers: Also known as floats, floating-point numbers signify real numbers. Floats are written with a decimal point that segregates the integer from the fractional numbers.

In [ ]:
a=0.33
print(type(a))

<class 'float'>

3. Complex numbers: Complex numbers are pairs of real and imaginary numbers. They take the form a + bJ where a is a float and the real part of the complex number. On the other side is bJ where b is a float and J or its lowercase indicates the square root of an imaginary number, -1. This makes b the imaginary part of the complex number.

In [ ]:
a=2+7J
print(type(a))

<class 'complex'>
In [ ]:
a=complex(2,-4)
print(a)

(2-4j)

# a & b = 1010
#          &
#         0100
#       = 0000
#       = 0 (Decimal)

Bitwise not¶

In [ ]:

# ~a = ~0110 = 1001 (Binary value of 9)

Bitwise Left Shift¶
